T-Virus

The T-Virus is Umbrella Corporation’s most ambitious breakthrough in genetic engineering, designed to enhance cellular regeneration and unlock untapped biological potential. Originally developed to revolutionize medicine and human performance, the virus can accelerate healing, strengthen immunity, and even alter genetic traits. However, its effects are unpredictable and often catastrophic: subjects may experience uncontrollable mutations, heightened aggression, and transformation into grotesque forms. While offering unprecedented scientific possibilities, the T-Virus also highlights the ethical and existential risks of playing with the very building blocks of life—risks that Umbrella relentlessly seeks to manage, contain, and exploit for its own ends.

G-Virus

The G-Virus pushes the limits of genetic evolution, creating rapid cellular mutation and extraordinary adaptability. Its potential for scientific advancement is matched only by the intensity of its power, making it one of Umbrella’s most formidable innovations.

G-Virus

The G-Virus represents the pinnacle of Umbrella Corporation’s experimentation in genetic evolution. Unlike the T-Virus, it does not just enhance cells—it rewrites them entirely, driving rapid and often uncontrollable mutations. Subjects gain extraordinary adaptability, accelerated healing, and sometimes new physical abilities, but these changes come at a terrifying cost: mental instability, aggressive instincts, and grotesque, unpredictable transformations. The G-Virus exemplifies the extreme edge of biotechnological innovation, where the promise of evolution collides with the threat of chaos, making it one of Umbrella’s most dangerous and awe-inspiring creations.

C-Virus

C-Virus

The C-Virus represents the next generation of Umbrella Corporation’s bioweapon research, merging enhanced infectivity with precise control over mutation. Unlike its predecessors, the C-Virus is engineered to spread efficiently while maintaining targeted genetic effects, giving Umbrella unprecedented mastery over both its subjects and outcomes. Its innovations offer the potential for revolutionary medical treatments, such as accelerated regeneration and adaptive immunity, but the line between cure and catastrophe is razor-thin. Subjects exposed to the virus may experience rapid mutations, unpredictable physical transformations, and heightened aggression, reflecting the inherent dangers of manipulating life at such a fundamental level. The C-Virus embodies Umbrella’s relentless pursuit of perfection, where scientific ambition walks hand in hand with ethical peril.

T-Veronica Virus

The T-Veronica Virus represents Umbrella Corporation’s most refined approach to viral engineering. Designed to trigger controlled cellular transformation, it enables rapid regeneration while preserving cognitive function—unlike the chaotic effects seen in earlier viruses. The virus can restructure tissue, enhance resilience, and even adapt to environmental stresses, offering a glimpse into the next frontier of genetic science. However, its power comes with inherent risks: overexposure can lead to unforeseen mutations, psychological instability, and extreme physiological changes. The T-Veronica Virus exemplifies Umbrella’s commitment to pushing the boundaries of life itself, combining scientific precision with the ongoing moral and ethical dilemmas of manipulating biology at its core.

Nemesis / NE-T Virus

The NE-T Virus, engineered for tactical superiority, heightens physical strength, resilience, and regeneration. It embodies Umbrella’s pursuit of peak performance under extreme conditions.

Nemesis / NE-T Virus

The NE-T Virus is Umbrella Corporation’s latest tactical bioweapon, designed to push human performance to its absolute limits. It enhances physical strength, accelerates regeneration, and fortifies resilience, making subjects capable of surviving extreme conditions that would overwhelm ordinary humans. While its precision engineering allows for controlled activation and targeted effects, the virus is far from risk-free: uncontrolled exposure can trigger unpredictable mutations, aggressive behavior, and severe physiological strain. The NE-T Virus represents Umbrella’s relentless quest to combine scientific innovation with battlefield superiority, blurring the line between human potential and engineered monstrosity.

Spencer Mansion

The incident at the Spencer Mansion began as a routine investigation into a series of grisly murders on the outskirts of a remote mountain region, but quickly descended into a nightmare. Upon arrival, an elite police unit discovered the mansion was a front for secret biological experiments involving a highly contagious virus that reanimated the dead and mutated living organisms into violent creatures. Trapped inside, the team faced deadly traps, limited resources, and the constant threat of infection while uncovering evidence of corporate corruption and scientific recklessness. As the truth emerged, it became clear that the outbreak was no accident, but the result of deliberate experimentation that spiraled out of control, turning the mansion into a sealed laboratory of horror.

Racoon City Incident - 1998

A catastrophic viral outbreak transformed a bustling city into chaos, as infected citizens became violent and uncontrollable. Emergency response efforts collapsed under the scale of the contagion, exposing the devastating consequences of corporate experimentation and biological weapons falling into the wrong hands.

Racoon City

The Raccoon City incident began as a covert outbreak of a highly experimental virus, initially confined to secret laboratories but quickly spreading beyond control. Within days, the virus infected a significant portion of the population, turning ordinary citizens into violent, uncontrollable creatures while overwhelming local law enforcement and emergency services. The city descended into panic as communication networks failed and streets became battlegrounds, leaving survivors to navigate collapsing infrastructure, blocked escape routes, and unpredictable threats. Evidence later revealed that the outbreak was the result of corporate ambition and unethical experimentation, with deliberate attempts to cover up the disaster worsening the crisis. What started as an isolated containment breach escalated into a full-scale urban nightmare, demonstrating the catastrophic potential of unchecked bioengineering and the fragility of societal order when faced with a viral epidemic.
